The Samurai were the elite warrior class of ancient Japan, known for their unmatched discipline, loyalty, and mastery of the sword. Emerging around the 10th century, they served as protectors of noble families and later became powerful political figures shaping Japans history for nearly 700 years.

In the soul of every warrior, art and battle coexist.

They adhered to a strict code of conduct known as Bushidō,
which emphasized loyalty, honor, and martial skill.
